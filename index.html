📚 EDUCATION

What is DNS Abuse?

As defined by ICANN, DNS abuse covers five core harmful activities that exploit the Domain Name System.

01
🎣

Phishing

Fake websites disguised as trusted services (banks, government sites) to steal passwords and personal data. Common in Nepal targeting NIC Asia, Nabil Bank, and eSewa users.

Example: nicasia-login.xyz
02
🦠

Malware

Domains that distribute malicious software when visited. These can silently infect devices, steal data, or make computers part of botnets.

Example: free-download-nepal.tk
03
📧

Spam

Domains used to send bulk unsolicited emails. Often used to promote scams, fake job offers, or carry malware links targeting Nepali users abroad.

Example: job-offer-qatar.ml
04
💻

Botnets

Command-and-control domains that coordinate networks of infected machines. These can be used for DDoS attacks or data theft.

Example: cmd-ctrl-server.pw
05
🧒

Child Safety Abuse

Domains hosting illegal content involving minors. ICANN and registrars work with law enforcement worldwide to mitigate these domains immediately.

Reported to: NCMEC & Interpol
ICANN

ICANN's DNS Abuse Mitigation Program

ICANN enforces DNS abuse obligations through its Contractual Compliance program. Between April 2024 and August 2025, ICANN initiated 400 investigations, resulting in the mitigation of nearly 20,000 malicious domains. This tool is inspired by and aligned with ICANN's mission to secure the global DNS for everyone — including users in Nepal.

Common DNS Abuse Patterns in Nepal

⚠️
Fake Banking Portals

Domains like nabilbank-login.com or nicasia-secure.xyz trick users into entering credentials.

⚠️
Fake Government Services

Domains mimicking nagarikta.gov.np or passport.gov.np to steal identity information.

⚠️
Mobile Recharge Scams

Sites pretending to offer free Ncell/NTC recharge, harvesting phone numbers and OTPs.

✅
How to Stay Safe

Always verify the domain carefully. Official Nepali government sites use .gov.np. Banks use verified .com.np or official domains.
